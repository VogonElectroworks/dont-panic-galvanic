# First Treatment: Technique and Procedure

Now it gets serious! Here you'll learn how to safely and effectively remove hair.

**Important:** This device operates **galvanically** (generating lye/NaOH in the follicle). Thermolysis and Blend are **not** supported. → [More on the methods](commercial-vs-diy.md#the-three-methods-of-electro-epilation)

## Preparation

### What You'll Need
- ✅ Disinfectant (70% isopropanol or skin disinfectant)
- ✅ Single-use needles (never reuse on multiple people!)
- ✅ Tweezers (fine, for extraction)
- ✅ Magnifying glass or headlamp (for precision)
- ✅ Lubricant (electrolysis gel or distilled water)
- ✅ **TENS pad (ground electrode)** (2mm pin connector) – completes the circuit through your body
- ✅ **Rubber band** (optional, see tip at the bottom)

### Setting Up Your Workstation
1. **Good lighting** (daylight lamp).
2. **Comfortable chair** (you'll be sitting for a while).
3. **Mirror** (if treating yourself).
4. **Sharps container** for used needles.
5. **Clean the skin:** Disinfect the treatment area *before* starting!

---

## Step 1: Disinfect the Skin

Before even touching the needle:
1. **Wash** (with water and mild soap).
2. **Disinfect** (70% isopropanol on a cloth, wipe the skin).
3. **Let it dry** (alcohol evaporates quickly).

**Why?** Bacteria on the skin could otherwise be pushed into the follicle!

---

## Step 2: Insert the Needle (Insertion)

This is the most important skill!

### Loading the Needle into the Pen (Preparation)

Before using your first needle:

1. **Remove old needle (if present):** Simply pull the needle out of the D-SUB pin in the pen.
2. **Insert new needle:** Push the new needle F-Shank (1.25mm) first into the D-SUB pin until it audibly/visibly clicks into place.
3. **Check fit:** Gently tug on the needle — it should feel secure but not stuck.
4. **Disinfect:** Wipe the needle and D-SUB pin with 70% isopropanol. The needle must be sterile!

### Hygiene When Changing Needles

- **Use single-use needles:** Electrolysis needles are single-use medical items. Never use a needle on multiple people.
- **Don't touch the needle tip:** The tip and lower shaft should not be touched with fingers (skin oil/germs).
- **Disinfect the pen:** The front of the pen (where the needle protrudes) should also be cleaned regularly with isopropanol.
- **Disposal:** Discard used needles in a sharps container — or a sturdy container (e.g., an empty tin can with a lid).
- **Wash hands:** Before and after each needle change.

**Note:** After many needle changes, the D-SUB pin may become loose over time.
Simply order a new pin (LCSC C5393697, ~$0.10) and replace it in the pen.

### Technique
1. **Hold the needle at a 45° angle** to the hair follicle.
2. **Slowly and steadily** push into the follicle (don't jab!).
3. **Depth:** Down to the hair root (approx. 2–4mm, depending on body area). The needle should slide in easily — not loose, not stuck.

### Signs of "Correct Depth"
- **Resistance:** The needle stops sliding further.
- **Pain:** Slight pulling sensation (if too deep: sharp pain → pull back slightly!).

**Tip:** Use the **rubber band method** (see below) to keep track of which hair you're treating!

---

## Step 3: Attach the Ground Electrode

The circuit must be completed through your body — the needle alone won't do it.

1. **Prepare the TENS pad:** Make sure the pad is clean and dry. For reusable pads, slightly moisten the adhesive surface with water to improve conductivity if needed.
2. **Choose a position:** Stick the pad on a **hairless, clean, dry area of skin** near the treatment site — e.g., forearm, stomach, shoulder, or thigh. Close to the treated area is ideal.
3. **Check good contact:** The pad should lie flat against the skin with no air bubbles.
4. **Plug in the cable:** The 2mm plug of the pad should already be connected to the device's second jack (see [Final Assembly](final-assembly.md)).

**Caution:** Do not place the pad on open wounds, scars, tattoos, or sensitive skin. If you have dermatitis or skin irritation, choose a different spot.

---

## Step 4: Apply Current (Automatic)

With this device, everything is **automatic**:

1. **Press the foot pedal** (current flows, timer starts).
2. **Wait for the time:** The device switches off **automatically** after the set time (e.g., 10–30 seconds for galvanic, depending on current). The time depends on how much lye (NaOH) is needed for that hair (see [Calibration](calibration.md)).
3. **Safety feature:** You can stop the current *before* the time is up by **releasing the foot pedal** (emergency stop for pain or problems). Alternatively, simply pull out the needle.

### What Happens During This?
- **Galvanic reaction:** The device generates lye (sodium hydroxide) in the follicle, which chemically destroys the hair root.

---

## Step 5: Remove the Hair

After the current application:

1. **Wait 2–3 seconds** (tissue relaxes).
2. **Take the tweezers** and grip the hair **right at the skin surface**.
3. **Pull slowly and steadily** in the direction of growth.
4. **Result check:**
   - ✅ **Comes out easily:** Perfect!
   - ❌ **Resistant:** Treated too briefly (apply current again).
   - ❌ **Breaks off:** Unlucky — follicle destroyed, the rest will heal on its own.

---

## Step 6: Aftercare & Treatment Strategies

### Aftercare (Right After the Session)
1. **Disinfect the area** (70% isopropanol or skin disinfectant).
2. **Cool** (aloe vera gel or a cool cloth — helps reduce redness and swelling).
3. **No sun** on the treated area for at least 2 weeks (pigmentation risk!).
4. **No scratching or picking** at scabs — let them fall off naturally.
5. **Moisturize** (fragrance-free lotion) once the skin has closed.

### Pain Management

Electrolysis is uncomfortable, especially in sensitive areas. Some options:

- **Numbing cream:** EMLA cream (lidocaine/prilocaine) can be applied 30–60 minutes before treatment. Available over-the-counter in many countries. Follow the package instructions.
- **Cooling:** An ice pack before and after treatment numbs the area and reduces swelling.
- **Breaks:** Take a break if the skin becomes too sensitive. Shorter, regular sessions are better than one long painful session.
- **Lower current:** If pain is sharp rather than a dull ache, reduce the current. Sharp pain usually means the needle is too shallow or the current is too high.

!!! warning "EMLA and conductivity"
    Numbing creams can affect skin conductivity. Apply it **after** attaching the TENS pad, or wipe the treatment area clean before inserting the needle.

### Treatment Strategies

There are two common approaches:

| Strategy | Approach | Advantage | Disadvantage |
|----------|---------|-----------|-------------|
| **Method A: "All at once" (Clearing)** | Every 6 weeks, treat all visible hairs | Systematic, never lose track of a hair | Time-consuming (many hairs at once) |
| **Method B: "Sessions by time" (Flexible)** | Shorter intervals (e.g., every 1–2 weeks), but only as many hairs as you can manage | Less stressful, can stop mid-session | Hairs remain visible, no experience of complete clearance |

**Why 6 weeks?** 
Hairs have cycles (growth phase = anagen, resting phase = telogen). Electrolysis only works during the **Anagen phase**. After 6 weeks, new hairs enter this phase that were previously "sleeping."

**My recommendation for beginners:**  
Go with **Method B**. Treat only 10–15 hairs per session at 1–2 week intervals.

## Optional: Special Tip: Not Losing Track of Treated Hairs ("Rubber Band Method")

A major problem: After treating a hair, you immediately lose sight of it when reaching for the tweezers.

### The Solution:
1. **Let hairs grow a bit longer** (approx. 3–5mm).
2. **Wrap a rubber band** (or small elastic) around the tweezers so they stay **closed**.
3. **Procedure:**
   - Select a hair.
   - Clip the tweezers near the end of the hair (held in place by the rubber band tension).
   - Insert the needle next to it (caution: avoid touching the tweezers — short circuit risk!).
   - Press the foot pedal, wait, current stops.
   - Simply pull out the hair with the tweezers (it's already attached!).

**Advantage:** You always know which hair you're treating!

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence | Solution |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Too steep an angle | Needle misses the follicle | Maintain a 45° angle |
| Too much current | Burn | Start below 3 mA |
| Too short a treatment | Hair stays in place | Increase treatment time (see [Calibration](calibration.md)) |
| Needle not disinfected | Infection | Use alcohol! |
| Tweezers touch the needle holder | Short circuit | Keep distance (rubber band helps) |
