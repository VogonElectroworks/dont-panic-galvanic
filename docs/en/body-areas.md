# Treating Different Body Areas

Electrolysis works on **any part of the body**, regardless of hair or skin type. However, each area has its own quirks — accessibility, pain sensitivity, and technique.

The following overview helps you prepare for each region.

---

## General Tips

- **Tighten the skin:** On areas with loose skin, gently stretching it with your free hand makes precise needle insertion easier.
- **Small needles for fine hairs:** For very fine hairs (upper lip, cheeks), finer needles (e.g. 003) are better. Coarser hairs (chin, bikini line) can handle 004.
- **Optimal hair length:** Hair should be **about 2–4 mm long** — long enough to see the growth direction and insert the needle parallel, but short enough not to obstruct your view. Too short (< 1 mm): the needle can't find the follicle. Too long (> 5 mm): the hair blocks the insertion angle.
- **Start low:** Even if the table suggests higher settings — always start low and work your way up.
- **Foot pedal placement:** Position the pedal so you can reach it even in awkward body positions.
- **Mirror technique:** For the face and hard-to-see areas: practice with a blunt needle or toothpick first until mirror-hand coordination clicks.

## Face

### Upper Lip

| Aspect | Note |
|--------|------|
| **Pain** | High — very sensitive area |
| **Self-treatment** | Moderate (requires mirror, inverted movements) |
| **Current (start)** | 0.5 – 1.0 mA |
| **Needle** | 003 (fine) |
| **Tips** | Stretch skin over gums (smile or press lip against teeth). Use a mirror. Take short breaks. |

### Chin

| Aspect | Note |
|--------|------|
| **Pain** | Moderate–High (thick hairs = more current) |
| **Self-treatment** | Manageable |
| **Current (start)** | 1.0 – 2.0 mA |
| **Needle** | 003–004 |
| **Tips** | Push chin down to tighten skin. For beard growth: work systematically in a grid. Often requires higher LU values. |

### Cheeks

| Aspect | Note |
|--------|------|
| **Pain** | Moderate |
| **Self-treatment** | Manageable |
| **Current (start)** | 0.8 – 1.5 mA |
| **Tips** | Puff cheeks or pull skin to the side to tighten. |

### Jawline / Sideburns

| Aspect | Note |
|--------|------|
| **Pain** | Moderate |
| **Self-treatment** | Manageable (profile view in mirror) |
| **Current (start)** | 1.0 – 1.5 mA |
| **Tips** | Turn head to the side, tighten skin with your hand. |

### Eyebrows

| Aspect | Note |
|--------|------|
| **Pain** | Low–Moderate |
| **Self-treatment** | Challenging (near the eye) |
| **Current (start)** | 0.5 – 1.0 mA |
| **Tips** | **Extreme caution** — never point the needle toward the eye! Stretch skin along the brow arch. Only treat individual hairs, don't ruin the shape. |

### Neck

| Aspect | Note |
|--------|------|
| **Pain** | Moderate |
| **Self-treatment** | Moderate–Difficult (loose skin) |
| **Current (start)** | 0.8 – 1.2 mA |
| **Tips** | Tilt head back to stretch the skin. Keep current low — skin is thin. |

## Upper Body

### Underarms

| Aspect | Note |
|--------|------|
| **Pain** | High — lymph nodes, thin skin |
| **Self-treatment** | Moderate (arm must be well positioned) |
| **Current (start)** | 0.8 – 1.2 mA |
| **Tips** | Raise arm and place behind head. Tighten skin. Disinfect thoroughly (sweat + bacteria). Avoid deodorant before treatment. |

### Chest

| Aspect | Note |
|--------|------|
| **Pain** | Moderate |
| **Self-treatment** | Moderate–Difficult (tricky angle) |
| **Current (start)** | 1.0 – 2.0 mA |
| **Tips** | Stretch skin. Treat while lying down for better access. |

### Stomach

| Aspect | Note |
|--------|------|
| **Pain** | Low–Moderate |
| **Self-treatment** | Manageable |
| **Current (start)** | 1.0 – 2.0 mA |
| **Tips** | Large area, many hairs — divide into sectors. Treat while lying down. |

### Back

| Aspect | Note |
|--------|------|
| **Pain** | Low (fewer nerve endings) |
| **Self-treatment** | **Very difficult** — hardly visible |
| **Current (start)** | 1.0 – 2.0 mA |
| **Tips** | Almost impossible alone — use mirror system or get help. Alternative: pro for the back, DIY for the rest. |

### Shoulders

| Aspect | Note |
|--------|------|
| **Pain** | Low |
| **Self-treatment** | Difficult (hard to see) |
| **Current (start)** | 1.0 – 1.5 mA |
| **Tips** | Place two mirrors opposite each other. Or feel with your hand and work by touch. |

## Lower Body

### Bikini Line / Intimate Area – Front

| Aspect | Note |
|--------|------|
| **Pain** | Very high — most sensitive area |
| **Self-treatment** | Moderate (front is reasonably visible) |
| **Current (start)** | **Below 1.0 mA** |
| **Needle** | 003–004 |
| **Tips** | Stretch skin. Start with the lowest current (!). Take breaks. Work lying down with legs spread. Use a mirror for visibility. |

### Bikini Line / Intimate Area – Lower & Back (Perineum, Gluteal Fold)

| Aspect | Note |
|--------|------|
| **Pain** | Very high |
| **Self-treatment** | **Very difficult** — barely visible, cramped space |
| **Current (start)** | **Below 1.0 mA** |
| **Tips** | Rear-view mirror or live phone camera feed required. Work lying down with knees bent. Never insert blindly! |

### Thighs – Front & Inner

| Aspect | Note |
|--------|------|
| **Pain** | Low–Moderate |
| **Self-treatment** | Manageable |
| **Current (start)** | 1.0 – 2.0 mA |
| **Tips** | Inner thigh is more sensitive than outer. Stretch skin while sitting. |

### Thighs – Back & Upper Area (Gluteal Junction)

| Aspect | Note |
|--------|------|
| **Pain** | Moderate |
| **Self-treatment** | **Difficult** — the transition to the buttocks is nearly invisible |
| **Current (start)** | 1.0 – 2.0 mA |
| **Tips** | Treat while lying face down. Use mirror or live camera feed. Upper area near buttocks: help or a professional recommended. |

### Lower Legs

| Aspect | Note |
|--------|------|
| **Pain** | Low (front), Moderate (back of knee, shin) |
| **Self-treatment** | Manageable |
| **Current (start)** | 1.0 – 2.0 mA |
| **Tips** | Very time-consuming (large area, many hairs). Leg backs are hard to see — mirror or help needed. |

|--------|------|

| Aspect | Note |

## Tips for Hard-to-Reach Areas

If you want to treat areas you can barely see:

1. **Mirror technique:** Use a stand or hand mirror. For the back: place two mirrors opposite each other so you see the area indirectly.

2. **Live camera on a monitor:** Position a USB webcam or digital camera (e.g. an old system camera with HDMI output) on a tripod or flexible arm, and feed the live image to a large computer monitor. This leaves both hands free and gives you a clear, magnified view.

3. **Get help:** Ask a trusted person to assist (back, back of thighs, buttocks). Training takes only 10 minutes.

4. **Pros for problem zones:** If you can't reach certain spots — let a professional handle the hard areas and DIY the rest.

---

## Dividing into Sectors (for Large Areas)

Large areas like legs, stomach, or back are best divided into small sectors:

- **5 × 5 cm grid** (roughly the size of a palm).
- Treat **one or two sectors completely** per session.
- Note which sector was done (sketch or photo).

**Benefit:** You see real progress after a few weeks instead of poking randomly across a large surface.
